# A Thunder of Dragons — GN #1 ("Origins") — Page-by-Page Beat Sheet

**Adapts:** Volume One, **Book One**
**Role:** The pitch object — the standalone book that sells the whole franchise.
**Status:** Working draft for Louis's review (canon authority). Beats and page counts are a proposal.

---

## Format assumptions

- **Target length:** ~**72 story pages** (a clean original-graphic-novel trim). Book One is dense, so
  treat 72 as the center of a 64–80 range — we tune once art starts and panels-per-page settle.
- **Splittable:** if the franchise later serializes (single issues / Webtoon), this book cleanly
  breaks into **two ~36-page issues** — recommended break noted at the midpoint below.
- **Two narrative engines, cross-cut throughout:** **Jay's** thread (the small kid → the fighter) and
  **Remi's** thread (the feral girl → the caged warrior). Sean, Nick, and Carly get single "seed"
  beats so the ensemble is planted without crowding the two leads.
- **Narration:** adult-Jay's retrospective captions carry the book. Remi's thread uses her own
  distinct caption color/voice (per Louis's note in the script) so the two POVs never blur.
- **How to read:** each line is one page (`p#`), with the core beat and — where useful — a
  **[money page]** flag, an *(art note)*, or a *(pitch logic)* note explaining why the beat earns its
  place in a pitch book.

---

## What GN #1 must accomplish (the pitch checklist)

1. **Open on the payoff** — the '83 Shogun so a buyer feels the destination before the origin.
2. **Make you love two kids** — Jay and Remi, fast, through humor and heartbreak.
3. **Prove the heart** — the Remi & Ksenia love story is the emotional core; it must land.
4. **Plant the world** — one clean mythology seed (the buried craft), not an info-dump.
5. **End on a hook, not a full stop** — the "baby dragons" turn, promising the ensemble & the saga.

## ACT I — THE BOY AND THE FERAL GIRL (pp 1–24)

**Sequence 0 — Cosmic seed**
- **p1** — SPLASH. 10,000 BC: fiery red objects streak across the ancient globe; final image, a Harlem
  forest — early inhabitants at a glowing-red impact crater. Caption: *"Maps used to say, 'there be
  dragons here.'"* *(art note: this single page is the only mythology we touch until p49 — plant, then
  leave.)*

**Sequence 1 — 1983: The Shogun (cold-open action)** *(pitch logic: this is the trailer — lead with it.)*
- **p2** — A hooded figure moves west past the Apollo to a fortified West-125th warehouse; inside, a
  New Jack crime party. Narration threads equanimity / light-dark.
- **p3** — A fist smashes a fuse box — the party plunges into darkness; gangsters snatch weapons.
- **p4** — The silent takedown: spinning kicks and strikes none of them see. *"...trained our bodies
  for those moments when the darkness must be put down."*
- **p5** — A floor of bodies; a runner is shot on the stairs; meaner gangsters level automatic weapons
  at the door.
- **p6** — The specific Zippo ignites — **FLICK's** face; a bound, taped hostage: a young woman with a
  **white braid** (*we won't yet know this is grown Genee*). VOICE: *"Can't hit what your eyes can't
  see."*
- **p7** — Muzzle flashes into black; shuriken answer, dropping the crew; Flick drags the girl upstairs.
- **p8** — [money page] The darkness behind Flick glows **RED**; a flash of steel; his head rolls;
  blood-spattered **white Converse** step into frame. Caption: *"This was the night the darkness won."*

**Sequence 2 — The frame**
- **p9** — The "socials" debate: Vietnam? Jail? Pimp? Dealer? — *"Not even close."* Cut to Buster's
  barbershop, a geeky 12-year-old sweeping hair (*our narrator — but we don't say so*). *"To really
  understand, we gotta go back a ways…"* *(pitch logic: sets the mystery — how does this kid connect to
  that massacre? — that the whole franchise pays off.)*

**Sequence 3 — Jay's world, Feb 22, 1972**
- **p10** — Snow; every tenement window flickers the same blue glow. Jefferson apartment: Jay & Genee
  transfixed by *Kung Fu* (Caine & Master Kan; "grasshopper"). *(rights: candidate for an original
  in-world show — see breakdown's flag list.)*
- **p11** — Jade home from work, tired; "Kung FOOLS"; the kids buzzing.
- **p12** — Dinner: Jay's penny/"grasshopper" bit — *"Who's the master?" "YOU Mama!"* Bedroom button:
  *"Wouldn't it be cool if a place like that Shaolin Temple actually existed?"*

**Sequence 4 — Willowbrook (Remi's origin)**
- **p13** — Willowbrook, 1972, night: **Victor** (disguised as a janitor) photographs the horror.
- **p14** — He finds feral **Remi** (12) in restraints — *"I DON'T BELONG HERE!"* — and begins undoing
  them.
- **p15** — She kicks free; attendants converge; she fights like a wild thing.
- **p16** — [money page] She crashes through a window into the snow, over the wall, gone — Victor
  watching. *(art note: mirror the '83 Converse energy — a small body moving through darkness.)*

**Sequence 5 — Jay bullied / Flick**
- **p17** — Middle-school roll call ("Ja'Quaryous Jefferson"); the size taunts; dodgeball as a war
  crime.
- **p18** — **FLICK** in full (Zippo, switchblade, third run at 7th grade); he eats Jay's lunch.
- **p19** — Jay stuffed naked in a locker; **Paloma** finds him — *"Victory adjacent my bare ass."*
  Flick: *"Tell your sister I say she FLY."*

**Sequence 6 — Remi found / Lozen / Ksenia**
- **p20** — A dump scavenger finds Remi's frozen body. 1881: the **Warrior Woman (Lozen)** wipes out a
  cavalry troop. *(pitch logic: her powers are ancestral, not random — the hook under her whole arc.)*
- **p21** — Remi wakes in a hospital; the Warrior Woman in the mirror: *"Run."* She bolts from the cops.
- **p22** — Subway chase; she leaps to the tracks; a train hurtles at her.
- **p23** — Snatched off the rail at the last instant — **KSENIA** (15, Russian). Coat, food, curiosity.
- **p24** — Names: *"They called me Remi."* … *"where you come from is called — what?"* — *"Hell."*
  *(End Act I.)*

## ACT II — FINDING YOUR PEOPLE (pp 25–53)

**Sequence 7 — Sho'Nuff**
- **p25** — Church: the "Sho' Nuff" call-and-response; little Jay shouts *"SHO'NUFF!"* *(rights: the
  Sho'Nuff / Shogun link is the crown-jewel-and-crown-risk beat — flagged in the breakdown.)*
- **p26** — Street: *"the size of those dogs tell me all I need to know… I am Sho' — NUFF."*

**Sequence 8 — '181' + Sean seed**
- **p27** — '181' station: Ksenia's world; the "borrowed" bath (*"They call police. We run."*); the
  haircut reveals how beautiful Remi is.
- **p28** — The clothes heist ("RUN!"); *Oliver Twist* as their shared language.
- **p29** — Chinatown: the Fengs called to school — **SEAN** (13) suspended for flooring a racist.
- **p30** — The curio shop, "GO BACK TO VIETNAM"; Sean's fury vs. his parents' restraint. *(seed.)*

**Sequence 9 — Buster's / the franchise lesson**
- **p31** — Buster Mackie's barbershop; the wall of history; Jay sweeps for $10. *(art note: the photo
  wall is a splash worth lavishing on — it's the book's soul-of-Harlem page.)*
- **p32** — **Nicky Barnes** in the chair: candy → wholesale/retail → *"the FRANCHISE"* — the hustle
  blueprint Jay will copy.
- **p33** — *"Fuck with a nigga's name, fuck with the very essence of who he IS."* Jay, head down,
  takes it in. *(pitch logic: the book's spine — identity, names, becoming who you're meant to be.)*

**Sequence 10 — Remi & Ksenia bond (the heart)**
- **p34** — Central Park: Remi's group-home past + waking dreams; an 1881 riverbank Lozen flash.
- **p35** — Ksenia's story: the Moscow Circus, the tiger, left for dead — *"But Ksenia not die."*
- **p36** — '181', a cold night huddled together: *"I'm glad you didn't die." "Maybe is ancestor."*
  *(art note: warmest page in the book — bank the tenderness now; we spend it in Act III.)*

**Sequence 11 — Birthday**
- **p37** — Sylvia's: Jay's 13th; he wants karate money; gets a $500 savings bond + a compound-interest
  lecture. *(Jade's love language: the long game.)*
- **p38** — Genee's real gift: white low-top **Converse**. *"sorry I called you traitor." "I know."*
  *(pitch logic: the shoes rhyme forward to the '83 prologue — quietly devastating on a reread.)*

**Sequence 12 — Train yard**
- **p39** — Bronx train yard: graffiti masters; Ksenia tags "TRAPETZENKA 181"; Remi tags **"REMI…?"** —
  the question that defines her.
- **p40** — Cops raid; the foot chase; Remi's ankle goes; Ksenia won't leave her.
- **p41** — They steal a police car. *"One for all, all for TWO!"* *(the joy peak of their romance.)*

**Sequence 13 — Candy hustle / Chuy / the pigeon**
- **p42** — Jay runs the candy play (the Barnes model), Genee his accountant, kiddie playgrounds off
  Flick's radar.
- **p43** — Flick's "peace": a live pigeon in a bag, soaked in lighter fluid — *burn it, or be burned.*
- **p44** — **CHUY** ("El Samurican") steps in: *"Nobody's lighting up anything… The Samurican Code is
  to stand against evil wherever he may find it."* A melee erupts.
- **p45** — [money page] Genee saves Jay (inhaler); Flick torches the pigeon anyway — it falls dead. Two
  battered kids, one alliance forged. *(Recommended two-issue break here — see note below.)*

**Sequence 14 — Nick & Carly seed**
- **p46** — A tae kwon do gym: **Nick** vs. **Carly** — Carly wins with a head-scissor flip; Egan's
  "Blacky Chan"; both kids clearly smitten. *(seed — the crime/romance thread of later books.)*

**Sequence 15 — The father / Chuy welcomed**
- **p47** — Jade's couch, two black eyes: the dead-father story — Golden Gloves champ, killed refusing
  to hand a mugger his $20 watch (*"his hands were faster than a bullet"*).
- **p48** — *"karate NO different!"* — yet she takes Chuy in: *"you are always welcome in this house."*

**Sequence 16 — '181' craft (the one mythology beat)**
- **p49** — '181': cats flee; a hieroglyph "map"; a ceiling mural — meteors, the crater, red rats, a
  dark goddess.
- **p50** — [money page] The cat-eating man: *"there be dragons here."* The half-buried **metallic
  craft** and its recurring symbol; glowing red rats; Remi pulls Ksenia away. *(pitch logic: the ONLY
  overt franchise-scale seed in the book — one image, unforgettable, unexplained.)*

**Sequence 17 — Buster / Wilt / destiny**
- **p51** — Black Belt magazine; Jay pays for a copy a kid stole — the first flash of his personal code.
- **p52** — Buster on Jay's father's demons and *"a day will come when a man just KNOWS."*
- **p53** — The man in the chair is **Wilt Chamberlain** (a fan of "Jackie Flash"): *"Ever been to the
  Garden, kid?"* — two tickets that will matter. *(rights: real-person cameo — flagged.)*

## ACT III — CROSSING LINES (pp 54–72)

**Sequence 18 — Remi's mark / "fly"**
- **p54** — Ksenia finds Remi cutting the word **LOZEN** into her arm: *"'Cause she told me to
  REMEMBER!"* Flash: *"I am Lozen. Remember."*
- **p55** — The swing set: *"Empty mind and fly!"* Remi soars the fence. *"THAT who you ARE." "Ya tebya
  lublu."* *(art note: the visual thesis of her power — freedom as flight.)*

**Sequence 19 — Seeds & threat**
- **p56** — Nick witnesses the diner shooting (Egan): *"Whatever you saw — FORGET it."* *(seed.)*
- **p57** — Flick tries to take Jay's Garden tickets at knifepoint — *"Or end up like yo' daddy."* Jay
  refuses; Genee is humiliated, seething. *(the Flick debt that detonates in later books.)*

**Sequence 20 — The death of Ksenia (emotional climax)** *(pitch logic: the sequence that proves this
world has a heart — protect its page count above all.)*
- **p58** — '181': *"Is it weird I wanna kiss you?"* Ksenia's tender first kiss — *"Now you know kiss…"*
- **p59** — Glowing red rats; the homeless man, glowing **RED**, attacks; Ksenia launches herself at him.
- **p60** — [money page] He hurls her into a concrete beam. *"You should have run…"*
- **p61** — Remi slides between his legs, cracks him; a train rumbles in.
- **p62** — Remi kicks him into the train — he splatters. *"You said not to TOUCH it!"*
- **p63** — [money page] Ksenia's last word: *"Survive."* A grief splash — the light goes out of Remi.
  *(art note: hold this page; it's the book's gut-punch.)*

**Sequence 21 — Fallout**
- **p64** — Carly's father is shot dead at "Sweet's Greasy Spoon"; Carly devastated. *(seed — the
  wound that makes her a cop.)*
- **p65** — Remi's courtroom — *"GLOWING Red Rats…"* — remanded to juvenile detention until 18.

**Sequence 22 — Jay chooses the path**
- **p66** — *"Comes a day when a man just knows…"* Jay walks into Takegawa's dojo, joins Chuy — *"AGAIN!"*
- **p67** — Training; Jay punches through a board (Flick's face on it): *"Kii-YAH, muthafucka!"*

**Sequence 23 — THE BABY DRAGONS (the re-pointed ending / the hook)** *(the single most important
change vs. the manuscript — see breakdown §3b.)*
- **p68** — [money page] Narration: *"The Chinese Year of the Dragon wouldn't officially begin for a few
  years… but a whole generation of baby dragons were being born all across this city…"* MONTAGE: Remi
  in juvi; Sean, Carly, Nick each in a dojo.
- **p69** — *"…Dragons I'm talking about here was us."* A wide ensemble tease — the faces that will
  converge. *(pitch logic: this page is the promise the buyer buys — the saga, not the memoir.)*
- **p70** — Home coda: Jay & Genee, candy and *Kung Fu* — *"Snatch the M&M, grasshopper." "Who's the
  Master now?"*
- **p71** — Button: *"Irony is, if not for Flick, I might'a ended up being a barber."*
- **p72** — [money page] FINAL PAGE / hook: a silent last beat that plants the future — the buried
  craft's symbol glowing faintly beneath the city — and a tag line teasing **Book Two / Enter the
  Dragonverse**. *(art note: rhyme the final image with the '83 Converse from p8 — end where we began,
  now understood.)*

## Two-issue split (only if serializing to floppies / Webtoon)

- **Issue #1 = pp 1–45**, ending on the burned pigeon / **Jay + Chuy alliance forged** — a strong
  emotional + tonal cliffhanger ("these two, against all of it").
- **Issue #2 = pp 46–72**, building to Ksenia's death and the "baby dragons" hook.
- For the four-graphic-novel plan (one book per Book), ignore this — GN #1 ships as one ~72-page book.
