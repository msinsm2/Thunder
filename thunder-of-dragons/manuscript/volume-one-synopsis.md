# A Thunder of Dragons — Volume One: Synopsis / Navigation Aid

> **This is a synopsis, not the manuscript.** It condenses each Book for quick reference and
> adaptation planning. The complete script text is in [`volume-one-raw.txt`](volume-one-raw.txt)
> (extracted from the PDF's text layer), and the original 178-page illustrated PDF at the repo root
> is the ultimate source of record. Use this file to navigate; use the raw text (or the PDF) to work
> from. The four internal Book divisions are the author's own.

## BOOK ONE

**1 — PROLOGUE: EARTH — THOUSANDS OF YEARS AGO...**

Many objects, maybe METEORS, maybe SOMETHING ELSE, streak through the atmosphere in a blaze. Early
humans and animals across the globe look up — Africa, China, Europe, Egypt, Sumeria, South America
(Olmec, Inca), and finally a dense forest, HARLEM, 10,000 BC, where early inhabitants gaze at a
glowing red object at the bottom of an impact crater.

**2 — CONCRETE JUNGLE, NIGHT — HARLEM, 1983**

A shadowy hooded figure heads west past the Apollo toward a fortified warehouse on West 125 near the
river. Inside: a New Jack crime operation. A fist smashes a fuse box; the party goes dark. A figure
flashes silently through the shadows, taking out gangsters one by one. Narration threads through on
equanimity, light and dark, the line one must not cross.

Upstairs, FLICK holds a terrified girl (21, Black — a white braid / albino spot in her hair), wrists
bound, mouth taped. A disembodied voice: "Can't hit what your eyes can't see." Gunfire into darkness;
shuriken answer. Flick drags the girl into a dark room, threatens to kill her. The darkness behind
him glows red — a flash of steel — Flick's head rolls. Blood-spattered white Converse step in.
Narration: *"this was the night I crossed that line… the night the darkness won."*

**3 — On the "socials," debate rages about Sho' (the Shogun): Vietnam? Jail? Pimp? Dealer? "Not even
close."** Cut to an old-school Harlem barbershop where a skinny, geeky kid (12, big glasses) sweeps
hair — BUSTER MACKIE and barbers at work. Narration: *"To really understand, we gotta go back a
ways…"*

Snow on a moody Harlem street; tenement windows all flicker with the same blue glow. **February 22,
1972.** Inside the Jefferson apartment: JA'QUARYOUS ("JAY") JEFFERSON and GENEE (pretty, 10, a white
albino spot in her hair) watch *Kung Fu* — Kwai Chang Caine and Master Kan ("snatch the pebble from
my palm… grasshopper"). Their mother JADE (36) comes home tired. The kids breathlessly describe the
new show; Jade jokes about "kung FOOLS." Jay holds out a penny: "Snatch this penny… When you can
snatch the penny, mama, you will be the Master." Jade snatches it. In bed, Jay wonders if a Shaolin
Temple could really exist.

**Willowbrook State School, Staten Island, 1972, winter, night.** A man (20s, brown, ponytail),
dressed as a janitor, photographs the horrors of Willowbrook. He finds a feral brown girl (12) in
rags — fierce eyes — who insists, "I don't belong here!" He begins undoing her restraints; she kicks
free and fights her way out, crashing through a window and disappearing into the snow. This is the
first appearance of REMI.

**6 — Middle school, Harlem.** Jay endures roll call ("Ja'Quaryous Jefferson"), bullying about his
size (Pee-Wee, Shrimp, Lilliputian), dodgeball, and FLICK — a mean 14-year-old who's done seventh
grade three times, with a signature Zippo and switchblade. Flick torments Jay (eats his lunch, stuffs
him naked in a locker). A Dominican girl (PALOMA, 12) finds him. Narration frames Jade's "victor
plays the long game… you're simply victory-adjacent."

**7 — The feral girl** is found near-dead at a garbage dump. Arizona Territory (1881): a fierce Apache
WARRIOR WOMAN single-handedly wipes out a cavalry troop about to kill an Apache woman. The girl wakes
in a hospital, sees the Warrior Woman's face in the mirror ("Run"), and flees — subway chase, nearly
hit by a train — until KSENIA (15, Russian) snatches her off the tracks. Ksenia shares her coat and
food. The girl gives her name: "They called me Remi." Where's she from? "Hell."

**8 — Church.** A Reverend preaches on temptation and the light within; the congregation calls back
"Sho' Nuff!" Little Jay shouts it out. On a Harlem street Jay asks Jade when he'll be big; she teaches
him about feeling small so he'll remember when he's not: "I am Sho' — NUFF."

**10 — Abandoned '181' subway station.** Ksenia cares for Remi; they bathe/steal clothes in an
occupied apartment ("What if someone comes home? They call police. We run."), read *Oliver Twist*.
A Chinese couple, the FENGS, are called to their son SEAN's (13) school — he knocked a boy's tooth
out for racist taunts. Sean is defiant; his parents preach restraint ("the nail that sticks up invites
the hammer"). Their curio shop on Mott St. is vandalized ("GO BACK TO VIETNAM").

**12 — Buster Mackie's barbershop.** Jay sweeps hair for ten bucks a month; the walls hold history
(Baldwin, Hughes, Coltrane, Ali, Malcolm X, Dr. King, etc.). NICKY BARNES is in the chair, telling
his origin: selling candy wholesale/retail as a kid, learning "the FRANCHISE." "Fuck with a nigga's
name, fuck with the very essence of who he IS."

**Central Park, Bethesda Fountain — Remi & Ksenia.** Remi describes the group home and her waking
dreams; more Lozen/Arizona flashes. Ksenia recounts leaving the Moscow Circus (trapeze; bitten by a
tiger; left to die). They grow close ("I'm glad you didn't die… Maybe is ancestor").

**14 — Lenox Avenue, spring.** Everybody knows Mama. Jay's 13th birthday at Sylvia's — he hopes for
karate-lesson money; instead gets a $500 savings bond (locked till 21) and a lesson in compound
interest. Genee gives him white low-top Converse All-Stars. Bronx train yard: Remi & Ksenia among
graffiti masters (TAKI 183, BLADE, ZEPHYR, STAYHIGH 149); Ksenia tags "TRAPETZENKA 181," Remi tags
"REMI…?" Cops raid; a foot chase; Remi hurts her ankle, Ksenia won't leave her, they steal a police
car and escape ("One for all, all for TWO!").

**Jay's candy hustle** begins (Nicky Barnes's model), with Genee as corner boy/accountant. Flick and
crew corner them; Flick's "peace offering" is to make Jay burn a live pigeon soaked in lighter fluid,
or be burned himself. CHUY TAKEDA ("El Samurican," 12, Japanese–Puerto Rican, glasses, homemade
samurai costume) intervenes: "The Samurican Code is to stand against evil wherever he may find it."
A melee; Genee saves Jay with his inhaler; Flick torches the pigeon anyway — it falls dead. Jay and
Chuy bond.

**High-school tae kwon do.** NICK EGAN (14, Irish) fights CARLY SWEET (15, Black); Carly wins.
Nick's father JACK EGAN is a casual racist ("Blacky Chan"). Nick and Carly are smitten.

**Church/home** — Jade won't pay for karate; reveals Jay's late father was a Golden Gloves champ
killed refusing to hand a mugger his watch ("his hands were faster than a bullet"). Jade welcomes
Chuy for standing up for Jay. Remi & Ksenia at '181': a crazed glowing-red homeless man; strange
graffiti "map" of the meteor apocalypse; a disheveled man eating a cat warns "there be dragons here"
and points to a buried metallic object with a curious recurring symbol.

**Jay** buys *Black Belt Magazine*; asks Buster about his father ("an inspiration… had his demons").
Wilt Chamberlain (a fan of Jay's dad) drops his wallet; Jay returns it; Wilt offers Garden tickets.
Remi begins cutting herself ("LOZEN") because Lozen told her to *remember*. Ksenia teaches Remi to
"empty mind and fly" off a swing set — "Ya tebya lublu." Jefferson family; Wilt's Knicks tickets.
Nick witnesses his father flee a diner shooting ("Whatever you think you saw — FORGET it").

Flick tries to take Jay's tickets at knifepoint; Jay refuses. Remi & Ksenia's first kiss at '181' —
then the glowing homeless man attacks; Ksenia is thrown into a concrete beam. Remi kills the glowing
man (kicks him into a train). Carly's father is shot dead at "Sweet's Greasy Spoon."

Jay begins secretly training at TOSHIRO TAKEGAWA's dojo with Chuy. Remi is tried — "GLOWING Red Rats"
— and remanded to juvenile detention until 18. Narration: a generation of "baby dragons" being born
across the city — Jay, Remi, Sean, Carly, Nick — all in dojos or juvi. Jay punches through a board
("Kii-YAH, muthafucka!"). Home, Jay and Genee play — "Who's the Master now?"

> *Irony is, if not for Flick, I might'a ended up being a barber.*
>
> **END OF BOOK ONE**

## BOOK TWO

**1 — Cold open, the future.** A majestic eagle over a Himalayan monastery (the Jin Temple); red
lightning erupts UP from within the temple, spiderwebbing across the clouds. Warrior monks gallop
out. "It has begun…" Then **1991-ish New York**: a menacing dark cloud rolls over Central Park and
Times Square (marquees: *Terminator 2*, *New Jack City*); the sun blacks out; blood-red rain; global
panic. The red cloud swirls over D.C., the Pentagon, Beijing (warrior monks vs. Chinese military,
bullets useless), Moscow, Paris, the U.N. (Boutros-Ghali calls it extraterrestrial). Five silhouetted
figures raise glowing red fists into the earth. A reporter finds TUPAC poolside, unbothered: "listen
to the sky for a thunder of dragons… a WUXIA nigga out there gon' step up." Red lightning incinerates
the reporter. "2PACALYPSE IN THE HOUSE."

**2 — Growing up.** Jay and Chuy (14 → 16 → 18; orange → purple → black belts) train at Takegawa's.
Chuy is brilliant and "off," obsessed with comics; angry that all the heroes are white — "I'mma just
make my OWN damn comics." His creations evolve: *Sofrito and Swoosh* → *Yinja and Yangsta'* (villain
BATSHITO; Genee inspires the dark goddess DARQUARYA) → *Enter the DragonVerse* starring "Tiburón
Kataña and the Shogun of Alpha Centauri" — a light-vs-darkness space opera where Kataña always has the
Shogun's back. Narration wonders if Chuy can see the future.

**3 — 1978.** Sean (18) with LIAN; the "dick-measuring Goldilocks" cafeteria rant. Spofford juvi:
Remi (nearly 18) counts her six years in months, weeks, days. WARDEN COCHRAN sexually abuses girls;
Remi is thrown into solitary for reporting it; she hides a boxcutter. Her caretaker VICTOR BERNARD
(the Willowbrook "janitor"/photographer) turns out to be Apache — reveals LOZEN was a real Chiricahua
warrior, his great-great-grandmother, and that Remi is "her" (the Willowbrook girl). Ksenia's dying
word — "Survive" — is what kept Remi alive.

Genee (16) is growing up fast. Nick (20) and Carly (21) — Nick clashes with his crime-boss father;
Egan warns him off "Blacky Chan" and hints at "a thing" the Feds are sniffing. JUNIOR (15) cuts school
with WOLFIE and CUTTY to a Bruce Lee triple feature; dosed with an "enhancer," he trips into a vision
of the ancient civilization of **XIA** — a King and a Queen Goddess of Light present their **golden
child**; Bruce Lee guides him ("Pay attention"). Chuy pitches Marvel — they love the title *A Thunder
of Dragons*. Harlem decays; Nicky Barnes is on Rikers; Flick rises as a new-jack crime lord taxing
and torching local shops. Cop TERRY "T-BYRD" BYRD works a burned body across from Buster's; the
barbershop "code of silence" holds. Junior's dream-vision; Master Lu and monks on the Empire State
observation deck. Jay and MRS JONES ("a thing goin' on") — Jay's older secret lover. Narration:
"we could not hear the thunder of dragons in the distance."

> **END OF BOOK TWO**

## BOOK THREE

**1 — Somewhere in China, 1928.** Three young monks — LU, YU, and JIN (20s) — see something blaze
overhead and crash. At a huge crater they find a strange metallic craft bearing the recurring symbol.
Fearless Jin climbs down and touches it; the craft glows red; a bolt of red lightning cracks up to the
sky and spiderwebs across the land — the origin of the conflict.

**The Madison Square Garden "Jungle Tournament."** Aaron Banks emcees; celebrity judges Ron Van Clief
and Sonny Chiba; a diplomatic debut by Master Lu and the Shaolin Temple (Hoon, Shih, Balo). The whole
generation converges: Jay, Chuy, Sean, Remi (with Victor and the Juvi kids), Nick, Carly, Junior. The
bracket runs 256 → 128 → 64 → 32 → 16 → 8, intercut with a high-stakes pool game, FLICK vs. T-BYRD
("miss one shot — it's inevitable"). Bongo the Mongol (Kublai Kayam) keeps knocking himself out trying
to headbutt granite. SWIFT DEER (Victor's father) reveals Remi's full lineage — Lozen → a daughter →
Remi's mother (Swift Deer's sister Remedia) → Remi. Carly is revealed to be an undercover cop.
Genee sneaks out and her night goes wrong (drugs, Flick). Jay and Mrs Jones's marathon (the "Zodiac");
Junior's arc; LAURA CHARLES (15, guitar) abused by Cochran, attempts suicide. Down to eight finalists:
Jay, Hoon, Sean, Remi, Chuy, Nick, Carly, Junior. In the finals, Hoon beats Jay. Post-tournament: Sean
is jumped by Italians; Nick is arrested (Carly & Agent Bearden's setup — planted guns); Arman Arkadian
is killed; Buster meets T-Byrd; Master Lu resolves to recruit the finalists. Book closes on Jay & Mrs
Jones ("Tongue Fu") and Genee slammed by a passing car.

> **END OF BOOK THREE**

## BOOK FOUR

**1 — Jay's rage.** A blood-red revenge fantasy of killing Flick in the pool hall is intercut with Jay
furiously packing ("Don't think — just PACK. I'd given Mama my word."). Genee is in the hospital —
assaulted and drugged (Flick). Cochran punishes all of Spofford. Jade learns of Genee's assault.

Master Lu's monks deliver **scrolls** recruiting the tournament finalists to the Shaolin Temple
(Chuy, Sean, Junior/Leroy, Remi, Jay). Jade tells Chuy: get Jay on that plane; do not tell him what she
knows. Nick offers the Feds "Tinsley" to save Carly. Remi, learning Cochran abused Laura, takes
Lozen's bow and kills Cochran with an arrow between the eyes; she frees Laura Charles ("keep singing")
and flees. Sean provokes and wins a one-man war against the Italians in a Mulberry St. crosswalk, but
loses Lian (she leaves by bus: "there is another you in there… full of rage that terrifies me").

The Café Figaro shootout: Tinsley kills Agent Bearden and nearly kills Carly; Nick kills Tinsley;
Carly reveals Nick's father Egan murdered her father. Nick walks; Carly can't pull the trigger. Buster
reveals HE is the one who gave T-Byrd the name — Flick is arrested ("Darkness wins when a nigga like me
know better don't do what needs to be done"). Junior is sent to SIFU YU (Master Lu's old comrade,
keeper of "the book" and awaiting "he who can stand against any new leader") and gives his real name:
**Leroy**. Master Lu waits at the airport; the chosen — Jay, Chuy, Remi, Sean, Nick — converge like
"Reservoir Dragons." All had been chosen. The red jet rumbles down the runway.

> *We were going to the Shaolin Temple… And we were about to find the fuck out.*
>
> **END OF VOLUME ONE**
