# A Thunder of Dragons — Volume One
## Adaptation Breakdown: Serializing Volume One into Graphic Novels

**Property:** *A Thunder of Dragons* — Volume One, "Enter the Dragonverse"
**Created by:** Louis Venosta
**Purpose of this document:** Dissect Volume One to determine how it is produced as a series of
shorter, sellable books. **The plan: four graphic novels — one per internal Book — with Book One as
the standalone pitch object for everything that follows.**
**Status:** Working draft for review. Louis is the canon and story authority; everything here is a
proposal to react to, not a change to the text.

---

## 1. Key finding — Volume One already self-divides into four "Books"

The manuscript is not one undivided mass. It carries the author's own structural breaks: four
internal **Books**, each with a distinct opening, arc, and an explicit "END OF BOOK ___." These are
the natural seams we cut along.

| Book | Timeframe | Spine of the story | Ends on |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Book One** (unlabeled open) | 1972, framed from '83/'85 | **Childhood origins.** Jay & Genee, *Kung Fu* on TV, tiny bullied Jay, Flick, Buster's barbershop, the candy hustle, Chuy / El Samurican arrives, Jay starts karate in secret. Parallel: **Remi & Ksenia** (Willowbrook → subway → Lozen visions → Ksenia's death → juvie). Glimpses of Sean, Nick & Carly. | *"Irony is, if not for Flick, I might'a ended up being a barber."* |
| **Book Two** ("VOLUME ONE – BOOK TWO") | 1972 → 1978 | **Coming of age + mythology + the crime web.** Opens on the **1991 apocalypse cold-open** (red cloud, Tupac, "a thunder of dragons"). Chuy's comics evolve: Sofrito & Swoosh → Yinja & Yangsta → **Enter the DragonVerse**. Sean/Lian; Remi, Warden Cochran & Victor (Lozen reveal begins); Nick/Carly/Egan; Junior's Bruce Lee vision of **Xia**; Chuy's Marvel meeting; Flick's rise; T-Byrd; Mrs Jones. | Monks atop the Empire State: *"we could not hear the thunder of dragons in the distance…"* |
| **Book Three** (unlabeled open) | 1928 + 1978 | **The Tournament.** Opens 1928 China (Lu/Yu/**Jin** find the craft — origin of the red-energy conflict). Then the **Madison Square Garden Jungle Tournament** — the great convergence of the whole cast, intercut with Flick vs. T-Byrd's pool game, the Remi/Swift Deer lineage reveal, Carly-is-a-cop, Genee's night going wrong. | "END OF BOOK THREE" (Genee slammed by a car / "Tongue Fu") |
| **Book Four** ("VOLUME ONE – BOOK FOUR") | 1978 | **The Choosing.** Jay's revenge-rage vs. Jade's plea; Genee in hospital; **Master Lu's scrolls recruit the finalists**; Remi kills Cochran with Lozen's bow & frees Laura; Sean's war & Lian's departure; the Nick/Carly/Tinsley/Bearden shootout; Buster revealed as the one who took down Flick; Junior = **Leroy**; the chosen board the red jet to China. | *"We were going to the Shaolin Temple… And we were about to find the fuck out."* |

**Macro shape:** Books One–Two are *origins* (parallel arcs across years). Books Three–Four are
*convergence* (one span, 1978: the tournament and its violent aftermath). That is a clean three-act
engine sitting underneath the four books.

## 2. The plan — four Books → four graphic novels

**One graphic novel per internal Book.** Each cut falls exactly on a line Louis already wrote, so
canon stays intact, the edit is minimal, and every book gets its own clean arc and cliffhanger.

| Graphic Novel | Adapts | Working title | Role | Approx. length |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **GN #1** | Book One | *Origins* | **The pitch object.** Stands alone; sells the world. | ~72 pp |
| **GN #2** | Book Two | *Coming of Age* | Deepens the ensemble; plants the mythology and the crime web. | ~78 pp |
| **GN #3** | Book Three | *The Tournament* | The great convergence; secrets surface; the night turns. | ~64 pp |
| **GN #4** | Book Four | *The Choosing* | The climax and the launch into the wider Dragonverse. | ~48 pp |

**Why four:** the books come out evenly sized, each ends on a strong cliffhanger (the "baby dragons"
turn; the storm unheard; **Genee slammed by a car**; the jet to China), and no single volume has to
carry a double-length finale. Book One remains the pitch object that opens the doors for the rest.

Page-by-page beat sheets exist for all four in [`beat-sheets/`](beat-sheets/).

## 3. GN #1 — making the pitch object sell the whole franchise

Book One is the right pitch object. Three sharpening moves:

**a) Lead with the "buy-the-world" sequence you already have.**
The **1983 prologue** — the Shogun of Harlem carving through Flick's warehouse in silence; the Zippo;
the shuriken; the blood-spattered white Converse; the red glow — *is* the trailer. It promises the
payoff (who this small, bullied kid becomes) before we meet the kid. Keep it as the cold open; it is
the single strongest sequence for a buyer's room.

**b) Re-point the ending so #1 hooks forward.**
Book One currently buttons on the soft, lovely *"…might'a ended up being a barber."* Strong emotional
note, weak sales hook. The fix already exists a few pages earlier — the **"baby dragons" montage**:

> *"The Chinese Year of the Dragon wouldn't officially begin for a few years… but a whole generation
> of baby dragons were being born all across this city… Dragons I'm talking about here was us."*

…intercut with Remi in juvi, Sean, Carly, and Nick in their dojos. **Move that to the final page of
GN #1.** It names the theme, promises the ensemble, and converts a memoir beat into a franchise
engine. Keep the barber line as the penultimate emotional button, then hard-cut to the montage.

**c) Protect the emotional core.**
The **Remi & Ksenia** subway love-and-loss is what proves this world has a beating heart, not just
kicks. Guard its page count in #1 even if other beats get trimmed.

## 4. Rights flags — the Phase-0 "remove third-party references" pass

Read with the rights strategy in mind. **These are flags for Louis and the team's legal review, not
determinations.** They run in parallel with the dissection; none of them block it.

- **Highest exposure — the childhood spine leans on outside IP.** The early chapters lean hard on
  ***Kung Fu* / Kwai Chang Caine / Master Kan** (quoted dialogue, "grasshopper," Shaolin), and
  **Bruce Lee appears as a speaking character** in Junior's vision. These are the emotional engine
  *and* the most protected borrowings — strong candidates to be reworked into original in-world
  analogues.
- **Crown-jewel / crown-risk overlap.** **"Sho'Nuff / the Shogun of Harlem"** is the direct tie to
  the 1985 film — the connection that makes this valuable *and* the most rights-sensitive. Worth
  explicit legal clarity before it becomes the marketing hook.
- **Real people used on-page** (cameo vs. depiction): Nicky Barnes (extended scene + dialogue), Wilt
  Chamberlain (scene + dialogue), Tupac (full scene + an album title), Sonny Chiba, Ron Van Clief,
  Aaron Banks; plus Gorbachev / Powell / Boutros-Ghali and the wall of barbershop photos (Baldwin,
  Ali, Malcolm X…). Period-flavor cameos of public/historical figures are often defensible; named
  living-or-estate figures given invented dialogue are the ones to vet.
- **Brands / properties as set-dressing:** Marvel / X-Men / Justice League (Chuy's arc + a Marvel
  office scene), *Terminator 2* / *New Jack City* marquees, graffiti writers (TAKI 183, etc.),
  Converse. Mostly minor — but the **Marvel meeting scene** does real plot work and may need to
  become a fictional publisher.
- **Real institution:** **Willowbrook** (a real place tied to a real 1972 exposé) is used as Remi's
  origin. Depiction of a real institution in a period piece is common, but note it.

## 5. Principal cast (quick reference)

- **Jay (Ja'Quaryous Jefferson)** — narrator; the future **Shogun of Harlem**. Small, bullied kid who
  grows into a fighter and Chuy's creative partner.
- **Chuy Takeda ("El Samurican")** — Japanese–Puerto Rican comics-artist prodigy; author, in-world,
  of *A Thunder of Dragons* / *Enter the DragonVerse*. Possibly precognitive.
- **Genee** — Jay's sister (albino streak in her hair); the hostage in the '83 prologue.
- **Jade** — Jay & Genee's mother; the moral spine (finance-literacy, "Sho' Nuff").
- **Remi Tirado** — feral Willowbrook escapee; descendant of the Apache warrior **Lozen**; abused at
  Spofford; the "cure" (Remedia).
- **Ksenia** — Russian ex-circus trapeze girl; Remi's first love; killed by a red-glowing man.
- **Sean Feng** — Chinese-American; brilliant, rage-prone; girlfriend **Lian**.
- **Nick Egan** — son of Irish crime boss **Jack Egan**; loves Carly.
- **Carly Sweet** — Black martial artist; revealed as an undercover cop whose father Egan killed.
- **Junior / Leroy** — pizza-shop kid; the Bruce Lee / **Xia** "golden child" vision; "the Chosen."
- **Flick** — the recurring nemesis; rising crime lord.
- **Buster Mackie** — Harlem barber, keeper of history, secret mover against Flick.
- **T-Byrd** — the Serpico-styled cop working Flick.
- **Master Lu & the Shaolin monks** (Hoon, Shih, Balo; Sifu Yu) — recruit the New York fighters.
- **Mythology layer** — the 1928 craft, red energy, the **Xia** civilization, the **Jin Temple**,
  Lozen's visions, and the future apocalypse the whole saga builds toward.
